SCIENCE AND TECHNOLOGY

The Faculty of Applied Sciences and Technology was carved out from the then Faculty of Applied Sciences in October, 2016. The reason for renaming the Faculty was to help introduce new technology-related programmes and also to properly manage the existing ones in the institution.

VARIOUS POPULAR DEPARTMENTS WE HAVE

Computer Science

Computer science is the study of computers and computational systems. It involves the theory, design, development, and application of software and hardware components of computing systems. Computer science encompasses a wide range of topics, including algorithms, data structures, programming languages, computer architecture, operating systems, software engineering, artificial intelligence, machine learning, computer graphics, databases, and networking.

Statistical Sciences

Statistical science, also known as statistics, is the discipline concerned with the collection, analysis, interpretation, presentation, and organization of data. It involves the study of methods for collecting, summarizing, and analyzing data to extract meaningful insights, identify patterns, and make informed decisions.

Food Technology

Food technology refers to the application of scientific and engineering principles to the production, processing, preservation, packaging, and distribution of food products. It involves the use of various techniques and technologies to ensure food safety, quality, and nutritional value while meeting consumer demands and industry standards.

Infomation Science

Information science is an interdisciplinary field that focuses on the study of information, its properties, representation, organization, retrieval, and use. It encompasses various aspects of information processing, storage, transmission, and management, with the goal of understanding how information is created, communicated, accessed, and utilized in different contexts.

Mathematical Science

Mathematical science, often referred to simply as mathematics, is the study of numbers, patterns, structures, and relationships using logical reasoning and abstract concepts.
